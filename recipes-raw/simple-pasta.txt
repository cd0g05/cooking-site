Sausage Pasta with Garlic & Onion
A simple, customizable sausage pasta that goes from basic to impressive with optional upgrades

4
servings
Ingredients
1 pounds Italian sausage (fennel or spiced)
2 shallots, diced
4 garlic cloves, minced
1 pounds pasta noodles (such as penne or spaghetti)
24 ounces pasta sauce (canned or jarred)
1 teaspoons salt and black pepper
0.5 teaspoons red pepper flakes (OPTIONAL)
0.5 cups heavy cream or butter
3 tablespoons fresh basil or parsley (OPTIONAL)
0.5 cups parmesan cheese, freshly grated (OPTIONAL)
1 tablespoons lemon juice (OPTIONAL)
Steps
1
Brown the sausage: Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Remove 1 pounds Italian sausage (fennel or spiced) from casing (if linked). Cook until deeply browned and cooked through, breaking it into chunks as it cooks. Don't rush this—golden-brown = flavor. Drain excess fat if needed, leaving about 1 tbsp.
2
Cook aromatics: Add 1 onion, diced to the sausage. Cook for 4 minutes
04:00
 until softened. Then add 4 garlic cloves, minced and cook for 1 minute until fragrant. If using 0.5 teaspoons red pepper flakes (OPTIONAL), add it now.
3
Build the sauce: Pour in 24 ounces pasta sauce (canned or jarred). Stir to combine with the meat and aromatics. Let it simmer on low heat for 9 minutes
09:00
. Taste and adjust with 1 teaspoons salt and black pepper. (Optional: add 0.5 cups heavy cream or butter (OPTIONAL) at the end for richness, or add 1 tablespoons lemon juice (OPTIONAL) for brightness.)
4
Cook pasta: While sauce simmers, bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Add 1 pounds pasta noodles (penne, rigatoni, or spaghetti) and cook until al dente (follow box directions). Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining.
5
Combine & finish: Add cooked pasta to the sauce. Toss gently. If too thick, add reserved pasta water a splash at a time until you reach desired consistency. Top with 0.5 cups parmesan cheese freshly grated and fresh basil or parsley if using. Drizzle with olive oil if you want extra richness.
Notes
Pro tips: Brown the sausage until it's caramelized—don't just cook until done. That's where the depth comes from. If your pasta sauce tastes flat, let it simmer longer with the meat to concentrate flavors. Always reserve pasta water—it's liquid gold for adjusting sauce consistency. Fresh herbs go on right before serving, not during cooking, so they stay bright."
